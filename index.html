Senior Thesis

Antimicrobial Resistance Governance in Bulgaria
Policy Feedback Analysis of the 2023 eRx Mandate

Applies policy feedback theory to identify the systemic drivers of Bulgaria's persistently high antibiotic resistance — among the highest in the EU. Using OLS time-series regressions on EARS-Net data (2006–2023), the study finds that governance quality, particularly rule of law, is a stronger predictor of resistance than healthcare spending or socioeconomic inequality alone. The analysis then diagnoses where Bulgaria's 2023 mandatory electronic prescribing (eRx) mandate is structurally vulnerable to reproducing the informal bargaining dynamics it is designed to displace.

Econometrics

A Smoking Gun or Just Smoke? Cannabis Legalisation & Substance Abuse

Co-authored study examining the causal impact of recreational cannabis legalisation on substance abuse admission rates in Washington State. Using a Difference-in-Differences methodology with Wisconsin as the control group, the analysis finds a statistically significant increase of ~310 admissions per 100,000 population annually attributable to legalisation (p = 0.033).

Causal Inference Methods: Matching, IV & Synthetic Control in R

R Markdown assignment applying a full toolkit of causal inference methods to real datasets. Used propensity score matching, genetic matching, and sensitivity analysis on the LaLonde job training data, instrumental variables (LATE via WALD estimator) on JTPA earnings data, and replicated a Synthetic Control analysis of Florida's Stand Your Ground law and its effect on homicide rates — including spaghetti plots and RMSPE placebo tests.

Comparative Politics

Causes of Declining Voter Participation in Bulgaria, 1990s–2020s

Systems analysis of Bulgaria's dramatic voter turnout collapse — from 90.6% in 1990 to 34.4% in 2024. Applies institutional (ADICO framework), structural (governance network theory), and actor-level analysis to explain how clientelism, oligarchic capture, vote buying, and weakening programmatic competition collectively produced the post-communist low-turnout equilibrium.

Statistical Modelling

Predicting Rental Prices in India: Regression Modelling in R

R Markdown assignment exploring predictive modelling on a dataset of 4,746 rental units across six Indian cities. Compared simple OLS and log-transformed regression models, evaluated train/test RMSE and 5-fold cross-validation, and analysed the effect of unit size, furnishing status, and point of contact on rent prices. Found that a log-transformed model with categorical predictors explained 58% of the variance — far outperforming size alone.

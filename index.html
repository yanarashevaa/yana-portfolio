About

I am an early-career researcher with a rigorous academic background in environmental science, climate modeling, and public health policy. My work bridges quantitative data analysis and evidence-based policymaking.

I specialize in geospatial satellite data analysis using Google Earth Engine, statistical modeling in R and Python, and translating complex scientific findings into clear policy recommendations.

Beyond research, I bring UX design experience — the ability to communicate technical findings to non-technical audiences is central to everything I do.

Analyzed spatio-temporal patterns of extreme precipitation events using Google Earth Engine and multi-decade satellite datasets. Applied econometric modeling to attribute trends to climate drivers, with implications for regional flood risk assessment and infrastructure planning.

Processed and analyzed aerosol optical depth (AOD) and PM2.5 data from MODIS and Sentinel satellite imagery. Developed geospatial visualization workflows in Python to map pollution hotspots and seasonal patterns.
